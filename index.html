Using this 3D surface plot, we compare log-scaled differences in keyword frequencies across eight security and development-related keywords over four five-year periods between China and the United States. The plot helps us visually track shifts in focus and relative emphasis from 2003 to 2023.

While public discourse suggests growing concerns about China’s intentions regarding war, this frequency-based visualization does not directly evidence a shift toward explicitly war-oriented rhetoric in China’s UNSC speeches. An increased focus on sovereignty does not itself indicate preparation for war but may reflect China’s interest in asserting its positions on issues such as Taiwan and the South China Sea.
